Baggage policy of American:

American baggage policy is designed with the passenger's convenience in mind, ensuring a smooth travel experience. For carry-on items, each passenger is allowed one bag and one personal item, exempting assistive devices and outer garments from the personal item count. These items should fit either in the overhead bin or under the seat in front, with passengers responsible for lifting their carry- ons without assistance. The American carry-on bag must adhere to specific dimensions (45 linear inches/115 cm) and weight restrictions (22 lb/10 kg). It's essential to note that bulkhead seats lack underseat storage during take-off and landing.

Checked baggage allowances vary based on the destination and class of service. For domestic flights within Mexico, Premier Class allows 66 lb/30 kg per bag, while Coach Class permits 55 lb/25 kg per bag. International flights to/from the USA and Central America have different criteria. Premier Class passengers can check 2 pieces, each with a maximum weight of 70 lb/32 kg and a size limit of 62 in/158 cm. In contrast, Coach Class passengers are allowed 1 piece with a maximum weight of 50 lb/23 kg and a size limit of 62 in/158 cm. This thoughtful and comprehensive policy ensures that American passengers can travel with ease and confidence.
